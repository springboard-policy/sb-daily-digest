## Skills Policy & Workforce Development

**Top Takeaways**

1. **C.D. Howe calls for flexible, innovation-first AI competition policy** — A new C.D. Howe Commentary (*Chipping In*, Daniel Schwanen) urges Canada's Competition Bureau to apply a case-by-case, effects-based approach to AI markets rather than rigid product definitions. Key recommendations: prioritize innovation market monitoring, improve SME access to AI infrastructure and data inputs, and issue clear guidance under the recently strengthened *Competition Act* to avoid chilling investment. The report frames government itself as a potential pro-competitive force through open-source support and expanded compute access.

2. **Ontario school violence framed as workforce/staffing crisis** — CCPA analysis of a Toronto educators' research project documents chronic loss of educational assistants, child and youth workers, social workers, and specialist teachers as the structural driver of classroom violence — not student behaviour. Authors estimate $6.5B in cumulative Ontario education funding erosion since 2018, contrasting Ford government's $63M supply card announcement as symbolic rather than systemic.

**What to Watch**

- The Carney government's AI policy agenda: whether Competition Bureau guidance on AI markets is issued proactively or follows enforcement action.
- Federal response to the C.D. Howe's call for expanded access to compute capacity as a public investment priority.
- Ontario education funding negotiations ahead of the next provincial budget cycle.
