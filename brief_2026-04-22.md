## Skills Policy & Workforce Development

1. **[Agnico Eagle invests $10 million in Inuit Nunangat University](https://itk.ca/agnico-eagle-invests-10-million-in-inuit-nunangat-university/)** — Agnico Eagle and ITK President Natan Obed announced a $10 million private sector commitment to Inuit Nunangat University at the Canadian Chamber of Commerce Future of Business Summit, bringing total secured funding past $160 million; the Inuit-led institution is slated to open its first cohort in Arviat, Nunavut in 2030, with presences across all four Inuit treaty regions.
   *The scale of private capital mobilized — $160M+ secured before a single class has enrolled — signals a maturing Inuit-led post-secondary model that goes beyond prior federal commitments, and establishes a precedent for resource-sector-funded Indigenous education infrastructure that federal skills policy has not yet formally responded to.*

   *This follows the pattern of equity-targeted trades pipelines emerging provincially ahead of expected federal construction demand tied to housing and infrastructure — a more granular approach to trades workforce development than the national apprenticeship and immigration levers that have dominated federal skills policy debates.*

3. **[Statistics Canada: AI adoption and productivity in Canadian firms](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiggFBVV95cUxOdERKZ2gyYjlfQ0Fmb0E4NzZ5cXVZR3VPczBZdEZPd0VfaWJzRDE4WkgxOTNNai1lemVfRk94M1g5NC1uT3IxZ05NdjNrQTVHUVlrVmdUMDA4V3g1cFVVbnRhNkkyRDc0ZGh1OVZiUGhDVl9LTmhUanQ0cFFBMkdnUm5R?oc=5)** — Statistics Canada released a new analysis examining the relationship between AI adoption and firm-level productivity in Canada, providing the first systematic national data on whether AI use is translating into measurable productivity gains.

Howe's April 15 call for a competition-and-innovation approach to AI markets, the StatCan findings will directly inform whether public investment in compute access and AI infrastructure can be justified on productivity grounds.*

4. **[Statistics Canada: Childcare centre accessibility, affordability, inclusivity, and quality, 2024](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiggFBVV95cUxPd3o4M01iU2E2VkxzVUlCaUVoNm1wWloyb1lDT3FCSy1HSXdMSlA2bUZraEw1TDZPT1JrRWduWkgzd1FfVFdNYy1sNkdDRXlyTzE3cENGMlp5R1NiRE9xV3g0SWhzS0hzcHpwSHJmdWNRdnJGQjVjenh3cU5KR0xYZUd3?oc=5)** — Statistics Canada published new national data on childcare centre quality and access in 2024, covering the five key dimensions of accessibility, affordability, inclusivity, flexibility, and quality across regulated centres.

5. **B.C. launches Guide Line: chronic pain support for tradespeople** — The B.C. government and Pain BC have launched a $1.8 million confidential text-line service connecting tradespeople living with chronic pain to counselling, pain programs, and substance-use supports, citing that 21% of B.C. toxic drug deaths in 2025 occurred among trades and transport workers.
   *This is a concrete workforce-retention intervention targeting a documented mortality and productivity risk in the construction and trades sector — particularly relevant as all provinces face skilled trades shortages, and it provides a practical model for mental health and substance-use programming that sits at the intersection of workforce development and health policy.*
