## Skills Policy & Workforce Development

**Top Takeaways**

1. **Bill C-20 (Build Canada Homes) heads to HUMA committee — brief deadline April 17** — The House of Commons Standing Committee on Human Resources, Skills and Social Development (HUMA) is accepting written submissions on Bill C-20, *An Act respecting the establishment of Build Canada Homes*, with a hard deadline of **4:00 p.m. EDT, Friday, April 17, 2026**. The bill's workforce implications — including construction labour demand and apprenticeship pipelines — fall squarely within the committee's mandate. Organizations working on housing-related skills and trades development should act immediately given the tight window.

2. **NDP tables surveillance pricing motion — potential workforce and consumer data implications** — Newly elected NDP leader Avi Lewis is expected to table a motion in the House of Commons this week to ban "surveillance pricing" — the use of personal data and AI to charge individualized prices. While framed as a consumer protection issue, the motion signals a growing NDP focus on the intersection of AI, labour, and algorithmic exploitation that will shape the workforce policy debate. Manitoba has already introduced parallel legislation provincially.

**What to Watch**
- Pre-Budget Consultations (FINA committee) are open for briefs until **April 30, 2026** — a key window for skills and workforce development organizations to put labour market priorities on the fiscal record ahead of the next federal budget.
- With a Carney majority now confirmed, watch for re-tabling or acceleration of previously stalled workforce development legislation and ESDC programming announcements.
- The NDP surveillance pricing motion may animate broader AI-and-work policy discussions, particularly around employer use of algorithmic tools in hiring and compensation.

## Social Assistance & Income Security

**Top Takeaways**

1. **Carney majority unlocked — AFN calls for immediate action on First Nations priorities** — AFN National Chief Cindy Woodhouse Nepinak welcomed the Liberals' consolidation of a majority government (achieved via three by-election wins Monday in Ontario and Quebec) as a "key opportunity for momentum" on First Nations issues. Priority demands include strong clean water legislation with source water provisions, self-determination frameworks, and productive outcomes at the planned **Fall 2026 First Nations–First Ministers' Meeting**, where First Nations will set the agenda. PM Carney committed to this meeting at the December 2025 AFN Special Chiefs Assembly.

2. **Métis National Council marks 10 years since *Daniels v. Canada* — calls federal inaction "unacceptable"** — The MNC is marking the tenth anniversary of the 2016 Supreme Court ruling that confirmed federal constitutional jurisdiction over the Métis under s. 91(24). President Victoria Pruden warned that "a decade later, that responsibility has yet to be meaningfully realized" — Métis citizens still face persistent gaps in programs and services, and many Métis governments are filling those gaps without sustained federal support. The MNC is calling on Ottawa to advance a coordinated national implementation plan, utilize existing bilateral mechanisms, and invest in Métis-led solutions and self-government.

3. **Parliament Hill pressure mounts on forced labour supply chain enforcement gaps** — An all-party parliamentary group and researchers from UBC and McMaster convened on Parliament Hill Tuesday, highlighting that Canada's *Supply Chains Act* (2023) is not being meaningfully enforced: company reports are "boilerplate," no penalties have been issued, and the federal corporate responsibility ombudsperson position has been **vacant for nearly a year**. Researchers using AI analysis found filings scored an average of only 36% on specificity. With the U.S. pressing Canada on forced labour under tariff threat, this is both a human rights and trade policy vulnerability. Foreign Affairs Minister Anand has said a new ombudsperson will be named, but no timeline has been given.

4. **Senate committee studying women's health framework and Indigenous human rights** — The Senate's SOCI committee is studying **Bill S-243**, *An Act to establish a national framework for women's health in Canada*, and **Bill S-5** on health information interoperability. The Senate's Indigenous Peoples (APPA) committee has received government responses on both the *Respected and Protected* report (Indigenous human rights) and the *Missing Records, Missing Children* report. These intersect directly with income security and social assistance gaps for Indigenous women.

5. **PEI tables record $410M deficit budget** — Prince Edward Island's Finance Minister tabled a budget projecting a record $410-million deficit on $3.8 billion in spending versus $3.4 billion in revenue. While a provincial matter, it signals the fiscal pressures facing smaller provinces in maintaining social programs and is a data point for federal transfer discussions.

**What to Watch**
- The **FEWO committee** (Status of Women) is accepting briefs on *Abuse and Financial Vulnerability of Senior Women* until **April 17, 2026** — relevant to income security organizations working on elder poverty and financial exploitation.
- The Métis *Daniels* anniversary is likely to generate renewed advocacy pressure through the spring; watch for MNC formal submissions to FINA's pre-budget consultations.
- Carney's confirmed majority removes legislative bottlenecks — watch for movement on the Canada Disability Benefit regulations and other stalled ESDC measures in the coming weeks.
