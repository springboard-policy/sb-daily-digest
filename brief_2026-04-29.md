## Skills Policy & Workforce Development

1. **[Spring Economic Update: Liberals to invest nearly $6 billion in skilled trades through 'Team Canada Strong'](https://ipolitics.ca/2026/04/28/spring-economic-update-liberals-to-invest-2-billion-in-support-for-young-people-entering-the-skilled-trades/)** — Finance Minister Champagne's Spring Economic Update announced the flagship "Team Canada Strong" program: nearly $6 billion to push 80,000–100,000 young Canadians aged 15–30 into Red Seal skilled trades by 2031, with $16,000 per-apprentice grants ($400/week during technical training), employer wage incentives up to $10,000, a $5,000 completion bonus, fully funded trades training for Armed Forces Primary Reserve members, and more than a doubling of the labour mobility expense deduction for tradespeople.
   *This is the most significant federal trades investment in a generation — the program's multi-channel design (federal–provincial–industry–union partnerships, Armed Forces integration, and enhanced mobility supports) directly addresses the structural barriers identified in the April 21 FSC youth employment brief, and the 1.4 million trades shortfall figure cited by the government aligns with industry projections that have underpinned every FINA pre-budget submission on workforce development for the past five years.*

2. **[The Daily — Canadian Income Survey, 2024](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMifEFVX3lxTE5SeU5WTWY4UUNmNEtidWRDUHl0bmdxcUpxZUViMW9IdXhfU215TDNod1NLYjJMdVJKajdCa0piTDktXzc5Rko1b0RzblVoTjVNQnlWUkpfLWx1R2hwaHBLZmRzb1pwSUNqeVJFZUd2cnItMjFtZHZQZ0Q4M3Q?oc=5)** — Statistics Canada released the 2024 Canadian Income Survey, providing the definitive annual income, poverty, and inequality data for Canadian households.
   *The CIS is the empirical foundation for the Canada's Official Poverty Dashboard (also released today — see below) and will directly inform the adequacy debate around the Canada Child Benefit, Canada Disability Benefit, and GIS — arriving the day before the FINA pre-budget consultation deadline, it gives organizations a last-minute data anchor for submissions arguing benefit indexation and program design reforms.*

3. **[Canada's Official Poverty Dashboard of Indicators: Trends, April 2026](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMid0FVX3lxTFBOeVNSc093Rlk1cTFoSVdON1FCYlI2eHlyUEZybG9Na3k3UW5CcWJyeHN4MkFPX1RQUGljYWoxYzh4TUtodEF3dUFkTUdIanV6MFRLS25qQlhIQU13Z2RQUFJqLXJsbjZvZmZpVW1qczMxajNOdldZ?oc=5)** — Statistics Canada published the April 2026 update to Canada's Official Poverty Dashboard, tracking multi-dimensional poverty trends across the Official Poverty Line, food insecurity, housing, and social inclusion indicators.
   *The Dashboard update arrives as the Carney majority government prepares its first full budget cycle — it provides the authoritative cross-indicator poverty snapshot that advocacy organizations including Campaign 2000 (which submitted its FINA brief earlier this month calling for an $8,500 End Child Poverty Supplement) have been anticipating to corroborate or challenge their asks.*

## Northern & Arctic Infrastructure

1. **[Singapore-based company emerges as suitor for Yukon's Eagle Gold Mine](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiiwFBVV95cUxPYno1dF84aUxYSDhraFA3VjdzbXhmOG9fUDlvUE1VN2dIRkhzb3RkNWM2UWstRzIyV0taai13MERmMy1hb3BkZjRHRGpBTmhKRkhVTFZQZUNlcUd1cmhIUXlYbmdkdUNBSnFIcFYxMXJHT2lPOEhMdmhGTi1zT2ZBWkRmUDBTZjNmSTdj?oc=5)** — A Singapore-based company has emerged as a potential buyer for the troubled Eagle Gold Mine in Yukon, advancing the sale process the Yukon Government updated on April 28 following last year's heap leach pad failure at the Victoria Gold site.
   *A foreign — specifically Asian — buyer for a distressed Yukon gold mine will trigger scrutiny under federal Investment Canada Act national security review provisions, particularly given the mine's location and the broader critical minerals security context; this is also a direct test of whether Yukon's post-failure regulatory and bonding framework is sufficiently robust to prevent a repeat scenario under new ownership.*

2. **[Expert calls for action on workplace deaths in N.W.T. and Nunavut as workers mark Day of Mourning](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMi2wFBVV95cUxNLWxsdmdaWTYxWlBwbkRrNGgxNjVXN0lla285YTZjQXNtazhLSnU1VUh2OFZwbXhwcHd4TVJEekZxc09WWHNodmJzaS1ienlnMnpfOVpJTVVWWnRwOWtSb1U5MkxkaWRldm5RNWY5N3ZvY1IwTEhqVU9ZV0U1c2NPSUFtckZVRTJrcXNmaWtnNjlMR2o1MEFYdDFkYWNfV3cyakUwck9tV3B4amlOTTAtU0Q1ZlBQTE9NSFN2Q0Q2YjY4T2VzRDlLVE02T3FUT0xxYmtBcWQzM3p2Z28?oc=5)** — On the National Day of Mourning (April 28), an expert is calling for concrete action on the disproportionately high rate of workplace deaths in the Northwest Territories and Nunavut, where occupational fatality and injury rates consistently exceed southern provincial norms.
   *Northern workplace safety is structurally linked to the infrastructure and resource extraction sectors that dominate territorial economies — high fatality rates in remote worksites with limited inspection capacity and long emergency response times are an ongoing governance gap that the Carney government's northern infrastructure investment agenda has not yet paired with a corresponding occupational health and safety enforcement strengthening commitment.*

3. **[Bursary funding increased to support Yukon students pursuing health careers](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMingFBVV95cUxNUEt2SG5LdjRLYW1ZdFNjQTVoM2xnaE04ZWFxamdBZU5LUVFEQ3ZFSHBtRmNrZlZYNkl0QjZaVzhsTEtXT0FPektCV3FReC16QkM4ZVd2VFRpNDFWa29ibmVoQ2J0ampiWVVBM05KMVRnNHQzMXY4a1lhNWNRSmdrX0s1eXpDM0Z2SVdENWFFdTlNajBxaXZYQ3ZvR21sdw?oc=5)** — The Yukon Government has increased bursary funding for students pursuing health careers, targeting the persistent health workforce shortage that constrains service delivery in a territory where recruiting and retaining health professionals is structurally difficult.
   *Northern health workforce shortages are both a social services gap and an infrastructure constraint — communities cannot sustain permanent health facilities without personnel, and bursary incentives are one of the few levers territorial governments can deploy independently; this follows the April 21 brief's Inuvik teacher layoff story as another data point in a pattern of northern human-capital attrition across public services.*

## Social Assistance & Income Security

1. **[The Daily — Canadian Income Survey, 2024](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMifEFVX3lxTE5SeU5WTWY4UUNmNEtidWRDUHl0bmdxcUpxZUViMW9IdXhfU215TDNod1NLYjJMdVJKajdCa0piTDktXzc5Rko1b0RzblVoTjVNQnlWUkpfLWx1R2hwaHBLZmRzb1pwSUNqeVJFZUd2cnItMjFtZHZQZ0Q4M3Q?oc=5)** — Statistics Canada's 2024 Canadian Income Survey delivers the definitive annual snapshot of household income, poverty depth and breadth, and inequality across Canada, including the most current read on how low-income families have fared since the 2022–23 inflation peak.
   *The 2024 CIS will reveal whether the real-income erosion documented in the March 2026 CPI data (2.4% annualized, but compounding on years of above-target inflation) has translated into measurable poverty increases — if it shows deterioration, it substantially strengthens the case for immediate CCB and GIS enhancements that Campaign 2000 and Prosper Canada have been pressing in FINA pre-budget submissions.*

2. **[Canada's Official Poverty Dashboard of Indicators: Trends, April 2026](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMid0FVX3lxTFBOeVNSc093Rlk1cTFoSVdON1FCYlI2eHlyUEZybG9Na3k3UW5CcWJyeHN4MkFPX1RQUGljYWoxYzh4TUtodEF3dUFkTUdIanV6MFRLS25qQlhIQU13Z2RQUFJqLXJsbjZvZmZpVW1qczMxajNOdldZ?oc=5)** — Statistics Canada's April 2026 Poverty Dashboard tracks multi-dimensional poverty trends, food insecurity, housing affordability, and social inclusion alongside the Official Poverty Line — providing the government's own monitoring framework for assessing progress against its poverty reduction targets.
   *Canada's Poverty Reduction Strategy set a target of halving the 2015 poverty rate by 2030; the April 2026 Dashboard update will indicate whether the government is on track or falling behind — given the inflationary period and rising food bank usage documented across multiple sources this spring, the results will carry significant political weight for the Carney majority's first budget.*

3. **[Canada's Sovereign Wealth Fund: Investing for Future Generations](https://basicincomecanada.org/canadas-sovereign-wealth-fund-investing-for-future-generations/)** — A proposal published by the Basic Income Canada Network (by Ken Yang and Floyd Marinescu) outlines a framework for a "Common Wealth Fund" — a Canadian sovereign wealth fund targeting $2 trillion in assets drawn from natural resource revenues and corporate equity holdings — that would distribute annual citizen dividends of $2,600+ or lump-sum payments of $42,000 per adult after a decade.
   *The proposal lands in the same week as the Carney government's Spring Economic Update and amid ongoing FINA pre-budget submissions — while a $2T fund is politically aspirational rather than near-term policy, the framing of resource wealth as a basis for universal dividends echoes the Alaska Permanent Fund model and will find a receptive audience among basic income advocates making the case that Canada's resource wealth should partially replace means-tested income supports.*
