Northern & Arctic Infrastructure

  1. Naujaat hunters want to halt Baffinland's railway and port project at Steensby Inlet — Hunters and land users from Naujaat, Nunavut are calling for a halt to Baffinland Iron Mines' proposed railway and deep-water port project at Steensby Inlet, citing impacts on narwhal migration routes and marine mammal harvesting that are central to Inuit food security and cultural practice.

    Analysis: The Baffinland expansion has been a flashpoint between resource extraction imperatives and Inuit rights for years — this opposition from Naujaat hunters follows the pattern of Nunavut communities asserting land-use authority under the Nunavut Land Claims Agreement, and arrives as the Carney government's stated reconciliation commitments will be tested against its concurrent critical minerals and infrastructure expansion agenda in the North.

  2. Wekweètì hopes forest thinning can fuel community heating project too — The NWT community of Wekweètì is exploring whether forest thinning operations — undertaken for wildfire risk reduction — can simultaneously supply biomass fuel for a community district heating project, offering a dual-use infrastructure solution for a remote off-grid hamlet.

    Analysis: This is a concrete northern example of integrated wildfire management and community energy infrastructure — relevant to the April 24 Yukon-Ontario SMR partnership and the broader territorial energy transition conversation, and models the kind of locally-driven, multi-purpose infrastructure approach that federal northern investment programs rarely fund cohesively.

  3. N.W.T. agricultural discovery centre opens its doors — The Northwest Territories has opened a new agricultural discovery centre, a physical facility designed to develop and test food production methods suited to northern climates, supporting territorial food security and reducing northern communities' dependence on southern supply chains.

    Analysis: Northern food insecurity is structurally driven by supply chain costs — flagged in the April 23 brief's Churchill shipping cost item — and local food production capacity is one of the few policy levers that directly addresses the structural gap rather than subsidizing it; this facility represents a concrete infrastructure investment in that direction.

  4. Government of Yukon updates on Eagle Gold Mine sale process — The Yukon Government provided an update on the sale process for the Eagle Gold Mine, signalling ongoing efforts to resolve the future of the troubled Victoria Gold mine site following last year's heap leach pad failure.

    Analysis: The Eagle Gold site remains one of the most significant environmental liability and resource governance failures in recent Yukon history — the sale process update indicates the territory is still working to find a new operator rather than pursuing remediation alone, a distinction with major implications for northern mining regulation, environmental bonding requirements, and Indigenous consultation obligations going forward.
