Skills Policy & Workforce Development

Top Takeaways

  1. FSC study finds EDI backlash narrative overstated among Canadian workers — A new Future Skills Centre report based on a 5,603-person national survey finds 54% of Canadian workers view EDI positively, only 16% negatively, with support highest among economically secure workers. The findings directly counter the political rhetoric that has led some Canadian organizations to scale back EDI commitments in the wake of U.S. rollbacks.

  2. C.D. Howe: Express Entry improved immigrant earnings but temporary resident outcomes are deteriorating sharply — A new C.D. Howe report finds post-2015 Express Entry cohorts achieved near-parity with Canadian-born workers within five years, but non-permanent resident men's earnings dropped significantly from 2020–2024, with college-educated workers hit hardest. The authors recommend reverting to exclusive reliance on the Comprehensive Ranking System and adding field-of-study and prior Canadian earnings criteria.

  3. Ontario "Northlander Rideshare Pilot" criticized as below-minimum-wage gig work expansion — CCPA analysis exposes the pilot as a mechanism allowing Uber to pay drivers below Ontario's minimum wage under the Digital Platform Workers' Rights Act's "engaged time" loophole — with research showing drivers' median net earnings were just $5.97/hour in 2024. The provincial framework, set for broader rollout by 2027, has significant implications for gig-worker income security province-wide.

What to Watch

  • How the Carney government responds to C.D. Howe's recommendation to tighten Express Entry selection as it manages the politically sensitive pivot to lower immigration intake targets.
  • Whether Ontario's Northlander Rideshare Pilot triggers broader labour standards challenges under the Employment Standards Act or prompts federal interest in platform worker protections.
  • Uptake of the FSC's EDI findings by federal employers and departments navigating the political crosscurrents from U.S.-influenced anti-EDI discourse.
