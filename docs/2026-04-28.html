Northern & Arctic Infrastructure

  1. Naujaat hunters want to halt Baffinland's railway and port project at Steensby Inlet — Hunters and community members from Naujaat, Nunavut are calling for a halt to Baffinland Iron Mines' proposed railway and deep-water port development at Steensby Inlet, raising concerns about impacts on marine mammals and Inuit harvesting rights along the shipping corridor.

    Analysis: Baffinland's Phase 2 expansion — which includes the Steensby Inlet railway and port as its centrepiece — has faced sustained Inuit opposition for several years; this renewed call to halt the project signals that community consent has not been secured, placing the project on a collision course with the federal Impact Assessment process and the Carney government's stated reconciliation commitments at a moment when Ottawa is also pressing to accelerate critical mineral development in the North.

Wekweètì hopes forest thinning can fuel community heating project — The small NWT community of Wekweètì is exploring whether a forest thinning initiative — aimed at reducing wildfire risk — can simultaneously generate biomass to fuel a local district heating system, combining climate adaptation with energy security for a remote off-grid community.

    Analysis: This is the kind of dual-use community infrastructure innovation the federal government's Clean Energy for Rural and Remote Communities program is designed to support — it connects the April 23 Yukon Energy governance debates and the April 24 Yukon-Ontario SMR partnership to a different end of the northern energy spectrum, where micro-scale, biomass-based solutions may be more appropriate than nuclear for the smallest and most remote communities.

  3. Yukon government, Yukon Energy and ATCO Electric thank Yukoners for reducing peak demand this winter — The Yukon Government, Yukon Energy, and ATCO Electric Yukon issued a joint statement thanking residents for voluntary demand reduction efforts that helped manage the territory's constrained electricity grid through the winter peak season.

    Analysis: A formal public thank-you for demand reduction underscores how close to capacity Yukon's grid operates — the reliance on voluntary conservation as a grid management tool is precisely the vulnerability that the April 24 Yukon-Ontario SMR partnership and the April 27 CBC nuclear examination are seeking to address for the long term, and it reinforces the urgency of the territory's energy transition planning.

  4. Draft Community Wildfire Protection Plan published for Kluane Lake area — The Yukon Government has released a draft Community Wildfire Protection Plan for the Kluane Lake (Lhù'ààn Mân) area, adding to the territorial wildfire planning infrastructure ahead of the 2026 fire season.

    Analysis: The April 21 flood and wildfire seasonal outlook flagged above-average risk across Yukon; this draft plan is a direct planning response — wildfire protection planning is a prerequisite for accessing federal Disaster Mitigation and Adaptation Fund support, and the Kluane Lake corridor's proximity to the Alaska Highway makes infrastructure protection a joint territorial and federal concern.
