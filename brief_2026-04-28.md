## Skills Policy & Workforce Development

1. **[Closing the AI Impact Gap](https://fsc-ccf.ca/engage/closing-the-ai-impact-gap/)** — FSC Executive Director Noel Baldwin and a panel of Canadian AI leaders argue that Canada's failure to translate world-leading AI research into productivity gains is a talent deployment problem, not a research deficit, identifying low public trust, sluggish firm adoption, and weak academia-industry linkages as the three structural bottlenecks; FSC's role in the federal AI Strategy Taskforce is explicitly cited as a vehicle for codifying AI talent as a strategic national asset.

2. **[Building construction price indexes rise in Q1 2026 amid skilled labour shortages](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/260428/dq260428b-eng.htm)** — Statistics Canada's first-quarter 2026 construction price index shows residential costs up 0.6% and non-residential up 0.5% quarter-over-quarter, with builders in Atlantic Canada and the Prairies identifying skilled labour shortages as their primary market challenge; tariff-driven metal fabrication and structural steel price increases added to the pressure, while softened demand in Ontario and B.C. reflected buyer hesitation ahead of HST and GST rebate clarity.
   *The simultaneous presence of labour shortages in high-growth markets and demand suppression in Canada's largest markets illustrates the mismatch at the core of the construction workforce pipeline — the Carney government's housing ambitions under Bill C-20 cannot be delivered without resolving the trades shortage that this data now documents at the national level for Q1 2026.*
