## Northern & Arctic Infrastructure

**Top Takeaways**

1. **Kitselas Treaty Act introduced in B.C. legislature** — B.C. has tabled the [Kitselas Treaty Act, 2026](https://news.gov.bc.ca/releases/2026PREM0019-000416), launching provincial ratification of the first comprehensive treaty to advance since the Tla'amin agreement in 2016, after 33 years of negotiations. Federal Crown-Indigenous Relations Minister Rebecca Alty confirmed co-sponsorship; federal parliamentary ratification steps remain outstanding.

2. **ITK jersey in PM's office signals symbolic positioning** — PM Carney was photographed in his office wearing an [ITK hockey jersey](https://itk.ca/itk-hockey-jersey-in-pm-office/), a light but deliberate gesture of Inuit political proximity at the outset of a new mandate. **What to Watch**

- Federal ratification timeline for the Kitselas Treaty; neighbouring First Nations consultation requirements could surface objections before a Senate vote.
- Whether the new Carney government's early symbolic gestures toward Inuit organizations (ITK jersey, mandate letter language) are followed by concrete Inuit Nunangat Policy implementation commitments in Budget 2026.
- B.C.'s treaty-making momentum — if Kitselas advances quickly, it may accelerate pressure on other stalled treaty files in the province.

## Skills Policy & Workforce Development

**Top Takeaways**

1. **FSC study finds EDI backlash narrative overstated among Canadian workers** — A new [Future Skills Centre report](https://fsc-ccf.ca/projects/edi-backlash/) based on a 5,603-person national survey finds 54% of Canadian workers view EDI positively, only 16% negatively, with support highest among economically secure workers. The findings directly counter the political rhetoric that has led some Canadian organizations to scale back EDI commitments in the wake of U.S. rollbacks.

2. **C.D. Howe: Express Entry improved immigrant earnings but temporary resident outcomes are deteriorating sharply** — A new [C.D. Howe report](https://cdhowe.org/publication/canadas-immigration-gains-were-driven-by-selection-maintaining-them-is-now-the-challenge/) finds post-2015 Express Entry cohorts achieved near-parity with Canadian-born workers within five years, but non-permanent resident men's earnings dropped significantly from 2020–2024, with college-educated workers hit hardest. The authors recommend reverting to exclusive reliance on the Comprehensive Ranking System and adding field-of-study and prior Canadian earnings criteria.

3. **Ontario "Northlander Rideshare Pilot" criticized as below-minimum-wage gig work expansion** — CCPA analysis [exposes the pilot](https://www.policyalternatives.ca/news-research/ontario-is-getting-ready-to-hand-uber-a-massive-gift/) as a mechanism allowing Uber to pay drivers below Ontario's minimum wage under the Digital Platform Workers' Rights Act's "engaged time" loophole — with research showing drivers' median net earnings were just $5.97/hour in 2024. The provincial framework, set for broader rollout by 2027, has significant implications for gig-worker income security province-wide.

**What to Watch**

- How the Carney government responds to C.D. Howe's recommendation to tighten Express Entry selection as it manages the politically sensitive pivot to lower immigration intake targets.
- Whether Ontario's Northlander Rideshare Pilot triggers broader labour standards challenges under the Employment Standards Act or prompts federal interest in platform worker protections.
- Uptake of the FSC's EDI findings by federal employers and departments navigating the political crosscurrents from U.S.-influenced anti-EDI discourse.

## Social Assistance & Income Security

**Top Takeaways**

1. **B.C. records 115 unregulated drug-toxicity deaths in February 2026** — The [BC Coroners Service](https://news.gov.bc.ca/releases/2026PSSG0028-000411) reports 4.1 deaths per day, with 81% indoors, stimulants and fentanyl co-detected in over 80% of cases, and the Fraser and Vancouver Coastal health authorities accounting for over half of deaths. The newly added xylazine and medetomidine tracking in the provincial dashboard signals an evolving and more complex toxicological profile.

2. **Federal Access to Information changes could shield government emails and texts** — The Carney government's Treasury Board [proposal to redefine "official records"](https://thenarwhal.ca/canada-access-to-information-changes/) — potentially excluding "transitory" communications like email and texts — is drawing opposition from the Information Commissioner and public access advocates. The public comment window closes June 15; the change would materially affect advocates' and researchers' ability to track income security and social policy decisions.

3. **C.D. Howe immigration-earnings research has income security implications** — The sharp deterioration in [non-permanent resident earnings](https://cdhowe.org/publication/selection-matters-lessons-from-two-decades-of-immigrant-earnings/) (linked above) points to a growing population of workers earning well below subsistence wages with limited access to income support programs — a gap that federal social assistance architecture does not currently address.

**What to Watch**

- The Treasury Board's Access to Information consultation (closes June 15): civil society and think tanks working on social policy should consider formal submissions.
- B.C.'s response to evolving drug toxicity data — specifically whether the province re-engages with supervised consumption or safer supply frameworks given the shift toward stimulant-fentanyl co-use.
- Federal social policy response to non-permanent resident income vulnerability, as reduced intake targets may leave a residual population with limited safety net access.
