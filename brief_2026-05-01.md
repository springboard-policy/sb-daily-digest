## Skills Policy & Workforce Development

1. **Forestry grant supporting workers affected by tariffs** — BC's Ministry of Forests announced a $20.8-million forestry workers support grant — part of a $70.4-million federal-provincial Workforce Tariff Response initiative — to provide wage subsidies, rapid re-employment supports, skills training, and community-led job-creation projects for up to 1,400 forestry workers, contractors, and employers affected by U.S. tariffs and duties, delivered through the Northern Development Initiative Trust.
   *This is the first concrete provincial implementation of the federal-provincial tariff workforce response framework confirmed in March 2026, and its design — stacking wage subsidies, training, and community contracts — mirrors the multi-channel model at the heart of the federal "Team Canada Strong" trades announcement this week; it will serve as an early test case for whether the federal-provincial collaboration structure the Spring Economic Update envisions can deploy at speed and scale.*

   *This is a targeted, geopolitically-motivated credential recognition intervention — it inverts the usual direction of physician brain drain and signals that Canada's territorial and provincial health authorities are treating U.S. political instability as a short-term recruitment window; it also illustrates how credential recognition reform, typically slow-moving, can accelerate sharply when political urgency overrides procedural inertia.*
